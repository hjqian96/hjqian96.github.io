Various kinds of convergence of sequence of random variables

This article will states various kinds of convergence of sequence of random variables, i.e. convergence almost sure(w.p.1), coonvergence in probability, convergence in distribution and convergence in mean of order p. At first, we will state definitions for all above kinds of convergence and then we will see their relations. Secondly, Cauchy criterions for almost sure convergence, convergence in probability and convergence in mean $p$-th power will be demonstrated. Finally, weak convergence will be discussed and some theorems related to weak convergence will be proved.

Let $\{\xi_n\},\xi$ be a sequence of random variables on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F},P)$

Definitions:
- $\xi_n$ converges to $\xi$ in probability denoted as $\xi_n \stackrel{p}{\rightarrow} \xi$: if for every $\varepsilon>0, P\{\lvert\xi_n-\xi\rvert>\varepsilon\}\rightarrow 0,\, n\rightarrow \infty$.

Random Variable and Random Elements

Part I Random variables

Definition 1: A real function $\xi=\xi(w): (\Omega,\mathscr{F}) \mapsto (\mathbb{R},\mathscr{B}(\mathbb{R}))$ is an $\mathscr{F}$-measurable function or random variable if for every $B\in \mathscr{B}(\mathbb{R})$, we have $$\{w: \xi(w)\in B\} =\xi^{-1}(B) \in \mathscr{F} \tag{1}$$.
Definition 2: A probability measure $P_{\xi}$ on $(\mathbb{R},\mathscr{B}(\mathbb{R})$ with $$ P_{\xi}(B)=P\{w: \xi(w)\in B\},\quad B\in \mathscr{B}(\mathbb{R}) $$ is called the probability distribution of $\xi$ on $(\mathbb{R},\mathscr{B}(\mathbb{R}))$.
Definition 3: The function $$F_{\xi}(x)=P\{w: \xi(w)\leq x\}, \quad x\in \mathbb{R}$$ is called the distribution fucntion of $\xi$.

Stopping times

For this article, I will mainly demonstrate the definition of stopping time, optional time and hitting time and their propertities.

Definition

For a measurable space $(\Omega,\mathcal{F})$ equipped with a filtration $\{\mathcal{F}_t\}$
a random time $T$ is a Stopping time of the filtration if $\{w,T(w)\leq t\} \in \mathcal{F}_t$ for every $t \geq 0$;
a random time $T$ is a Optional time of the filtration if $\{w,T(w)< t\} \in \mathcal{F}_{t}$ for every $t \geq 0$.

Remark: Every stopping time is optimal and the two concepts concide if the filtration is right-continuous. Since $\{T<t \}=\cup_{n=1}^{\infty} \{ T\leq t-(1/n) \} \in \mathcal{F_t}$, so the stopping time is optional. For the next claim, suppose $T$ is a optional time and the filtration is right-continuous, then for every positive integer $m$, we have $\{T\leq t\} = \cap_{n=m}^{\infty} \{ T < t+(1/n)\} \in \mathcal{F}_{t+\frac{1}{m}}$, therefore $\{T\leq t\}\in \mathcal{F}_{t^{+}} = \mathcal{F}_{t} $.
