Stopping Times

For this article, I will mainly demonstrate the definition of stopping time, optimal time and hitting time and their propertities.

Definition

For a measurable space $(\Omega,\mathcal{F})$ equipped with a filtration $\{\mathcal{F}t\}$

a random time $T$ is a stopping time of the filtration if $\{w,T(w)\leq t\} \in \mathcal{F}_t$ for every $t \geq 0$.

a random time $T$ is a optimal time of the filtration if $\{w,T(w)< t\} \in \mathcal{F}_t$ for every $t \geq 0$.

Remark: Every stopping time is optimal and the two concepts concide if the filtration is right-continuous.since $ \{T <t \}\cup{n=1}^{\infty}\{ T\leq t-(1/n)\}\in \mathcal{F_t}$, so the stopping time is optimal. For the next claim, suppose $T$ is a optimal time and the filtration is right-continuous, then for every positive integer $m$, we have $\{T\leq t\}=\cap_{n=m}^{\infty}\{T < t+(1/n)\} \in \mathcal{F}{t+\frac{1}{m}}$, therefore $\{T \leq t\} \in \mathcal{F}{t^{+}} = \mathcal{F}{t} $.
